Quality Assurance    

Quality is paramount to K Mean Black. We actively work on improving quality assurance by applying modern work methods, encouraging factories to upgrade machinery, and not least, ensuring that proper processes are in place. We divide the quality process into two phases - The first phase refers to the pre-production process where the quality controllers take part in evaluating samples and fits, etc. The second phase is hands-on quality monitoring on the production floors. This includes, but is not limited to, initial, inline, and midline inspections. Final inspection is conducted when all goods are packed and in accordance with A.Q.L 2.5 or any other guidelines provided by our customers. ​ We frequently collaborate with external third-party entities to safeguard that our judgment is on par with market expectations, or when a customer wishes to use an external party. Inspection reports are always shared upon the request of customers. All garments are tested for the basic parameters at our in-house laboratory at pre-production sample stage as an additional checkpoint to third party labs/ test reports.

Sustainability    

For us, sustainability is a process, not just a product. A process that integrates sustainable production, manufacturing, processing, sourcing, distribution, consumption, and the business itself. We are dedicated to creating a better fashion future and we work hand-in-hand with fabric mills for new sustainable fabrics as much as we also collaborate closely with factories to educate them about the market needs.
                             India offers a wide range of certifications:
1. Better Cotton Initiative: The Better Cotton Initiative (BCI) promotes a comprehensive set of production principles and criteria for growing cotton in a more sustainable manner: socially, environmentally, and economically.
2. Fairtrade: Fairtrade is an ethical trade system that puts people first. Fairtrade offers farmers and workers in developing countries a better deal and the opportunity to improve their lives, as well as invest in their future. Fairtrade gives consumers the opportunity to help reduce poverty.
3. GOTS: Global Organic Organic Textile standard
4. Global Recycling Standard (GRS): The Global Recycled Standard is intended for companies that are making and/or selling products with recycled content.
5. OCS: Organic Clothing standard
6. FSC certified fabrics - Forest Stewardship Council is considered the "gold standard" designation for wood harvested from forests that are responsibly managed, socially beneficial, environmentally conscious, and economically viable, and later used for cellulosic pulp and viscose based fabrics.
